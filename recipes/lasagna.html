LASAGNA

Description

Lasagna is a hearty Italian dish made by layering wide, flat pasta sheets with rich meat or vegetable sauces, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and generous amounts of melted mozzarella and Parmesan. Baked until bubbly and golden, each slice reveals a satisfying mix of textures and savory flavors.

Ingridients

  1. 9–12 lasagna noodles (regular or no-boil)
  2. 2 tablespoons olive oil
  3. 1 small onion, finely chopped
  4. 2–3 garlic cloves, minced
  5. 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  6. 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  7. 1 teaspoon dried basil
  8. 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  9. Salt and pepper to taste
  10. 1 container (15 oz) ricotta cheese
  11. 1 egg
  12. 1 cup chopped spinach or parsley
  13. 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
  14. 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese

Steps

  • Prepare the tomato sauce with olive oil, garlic, onions, crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and seasonings (basil, oregano, salt, pepper). Let it simmer to deepen the flavor.
  • Boil lasagna noodles until al dente, then drain and set aside. (Use no-boil noodles to save time.)
  • Make the cheese filling by mixing ricotta cheese, an egg, chopped spinach or parsley, salt, and pepper. Add grated Parmesan if desired.
  • Start layering: spread sauce on the bottom of the baking dish, then add noodles, cheese filling, shredded mozzarella, and more sauce. Repeat until the dish is filled, finishing with sauce and mozzarella on top.
  • Cover with foil and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25–30 minutes. Uncover and bake for another 10–15 minutes until the top is bubbly and golden.
  • Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before cutting and serving.
