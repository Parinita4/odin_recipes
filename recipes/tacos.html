TACO

Description

A taco is a traditional Mexican dish consisting of a folded or rolled tortilla filled with a variety of ingredients. Typically made with either corn or flour tortillas, tacos can be stuffed with seasoned meat like beef, chicken, pork, or fish, and are often topped with fresh ingredients such as lettuce, cheese, tomatoes, onions, cilantro, salsa, guacamole, and sour cream.

Ingridients

  1. 6–8 small corn or flour tortillas
  2. 1 tablespoon olive oil
  3. 1 bell pepper, sliced
  4. 1 small red onion, sliced
  5. 1/2 cup corn kernels (fresh or canned)
  6. 1 cup cooked black beans
  7. 1 teaspoon cumin
  8. 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  9. Salt to taste
  10. 1 avocado, sliced or mashed
  11. 1 tomato, diced
  12. 1 cup shredded lettuce
  13. 1/2 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Mexican blend)
  14. Optional: sour cream, hot sauce, lime wedges, chopped cilantro

Steps

  • Warm up soft corn or flour tortillas on a skillet or directly over a flame for a slight char.
  • Prepare the filling: sauté bell peppers, onions, corn, and black beans with olive oil, garlic, cumin, chili powder, and salt until tender and flavorful.
  • Make fresh toppings like diced tomatoes, shredded lettuce, chopped onions, avocado slices, or guacamole.
  • Assemble the tacos: add the warm veggie filling to each tortilla, then top with your choice of fresh toppings and shredded cheese.
  • Add extras like sour cream, hot sauce, lime juice, or cilantro for extra flavor.
  • Serve immediately while warm, with a side of salsa or chips if desired.
